Prompt of the month

Rescheduling a lesson for a corporate client

Draft an email to a corporate client informing them that the lesson originally scheduled for Tues, April 21 has been rescheduled to Thurs, April 23 at the same time of 10:45. The tone should be apologetic but professional, suitable for a Japanese business context. Include a subject line, keep it under 120 words, and offer for the client to contact us if the new time does not work. Write the email ready to send with no placeholder text.

Why it works: it gives Copilot all four GCSE ingredients — a clear Goal (the email), Context (corporate client, Japanese business tone), Source (the exact dates and times), and Expectations (subject line, under 120 words, ready to send). Specific in, specific out.

01 · The basics

What is Copilot Chat

Copilot Chat is a conversational AI assistant — included in your Berlitz Microsoft 365 license — for quick help, drafting, summarizing, translating, and brainstorming.

Draft & reply

Emails, messages, and documents in the tone you ask for.

Summarize

Long threads, documents, and spreadsheets into key points.

Translate

Between English and Japanese, including Berlitz-specific work.

Analyze

Paste data or upload a file and ask what it shows.

Why it's the safe default

Your prompts and data stay inside Berlitz and are never used to train AI models. The simple rule: if it's safe in Outlook or Teams, it's safe in Copilot Chat  Start here for everyday work; if it doesn't meet a specific need, other tools can be used with the right settings.

02 · Access

Access & sign-in — look for the green shield

You can use Copilot Chat in your browser or as a Windows app. Either way, one thing confirms you're protected: the green shield.

In your browser

  1. Go to m365.cloud.microsoft/chat
  2. Sign in with your @berlitz.* work account
  3. Confirm the green shield is visible in the chat

In the Windows app

  1. Open the Microsoft 365 Copilot app from Start or taskbar
  2. Not installed? Get it from the Microsoft Store
  3. Sign in and confirm the green shield
What the green shield means

It confirms your session is running under Berlitz's enterprise data protection. No green shield? You're likely on a personal account — sign out and back in with your @berlitz.* account. Still missing? Contact BIS.

03 · Your first prompt

Your first prompt

A prompt is simply what you ask Copilot to do. The better the instructions, the better the result. The one idea to remember is be specific — and the fastest way to be specific is GCSE:

GGoalWhat you want — an email, a summary, a translation.
CContextWhy, and who it's for.
SSourceThe notes, text, or file to work from.
EExpectationsFormat, length, and tone.

Copy-paste starters — try one now:

Summarize an email threadpaste the thread first
Summarize this email thread in 3–5 bullet points. Highlight any action items, deadlines, and who is responsible for each.
Translate for workpaste your text first
Translate the text below into natural, professional Japanese suitable for a corporate client. Keep it polite and concise.
Get an Excel formulano file needed
Write an Excel formula that calculates the percentage change between column B and column C for each row. Show the formula and explain what each part does.
Be specific, not polite.

"Please kindly help" adds nothing — state the task directly.

Iterate, don't restart.

If it's not right, say "make it shorter" instead of rewriting your whole prompt.

04 · Do it now

The 5-minute starter path

Five steps. Do them in order, right now.

1

Open and confirm 1 min

Go to m365.cloud.microsoft/chat, sign in with your @berlitz.* account, and check for the green shield. That shield is your "safe to use" signal.

2

Tell Copilot who you are 1 min

Open Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions and add one line — you only set this once:

I'm a [role] at Berlitz Japan. Use clear, simple language and bullet points for summaries.
3

Run a real prompt 2 min

Paste a real email thread into the prompt window and ask:

Summarize this email thread in 3–5 bullet points. Highlight any action items, deadlines, and who is responsible for each.
4

Iterate once 1 min

Don't accept the first draft. Reply in the same chat with one change:

Make it shorter, and add a one-line suggested reply.
